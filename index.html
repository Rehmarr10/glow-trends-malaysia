Glow Trends Malaysia

This visualisation explores skincare behaviour, shopping patterns, product preferences, and beauty spending trends among consumers in Malaysia.

It highlights how skin concerns, purchase channels, product categories, and regional spending patterns shape the Malaysian beauty market.

Visualisation Purpose

Glow Trends Malaysia investigates how Malaysian consumers engage with skincare and beauty products. The visualisation focuses on common skin concerns, shopping channels, product preferences, regional spending, and product pricing.

The target audience includes Malaysian skincare consumers, beauty retailers, and marketers who want to understand consumer behaviour across categories and regions.

1. What Skin Concerns Are Most Common?

This chart shows the most common skin concerns among Malaysian skincare consumers.

Acne, pigmentation, dryness, and sensitivity appear as important skincare concerns. This suggests demand for products linked to oil control, brightening, hydration, and skin barrier repair.

2. Where Do Consumers Buy Skincare?

This donut chart shows where Malaysians commonly buy skincare products.

Purchase channels show how skincare access is shaped by online platforms and pharmacy retailers. This helps explain the role of convenience and trust.

3. Do Higher Prices Mean Better Ratings?

This scatter plot compares product prices with customer ratings.

Click a product category in the legend to focus the scatter plot. This helps compare whether higher-priced products are strongly linked to better ratings.

4. How Do Skin Concerns Differ by Region?

This heatmap shows how skin concerns differ across Malaysian regions.

The heatmap supports regional comparison by showing where certain concerns appear more frequently. This helps connect skincare demand with location.

5. Regional Skincare Spending Map

This proportional symbol map shows average skincare spending across Malaysia.

Regional spending patterns suggest that skincare behaviour is not uniform. Higher spending regions may reflect urban demand, product access, or stronger retail exposure.

6. How Product Categories Differ Across Platforms

This stacked bar chart compares beauty product categories across shopping platforms.

Product categories across platforms show how online and retail channels may appeal to different beauty shopping needs.

7. Which Product Types Are Preferred?

This horizontal bar chart shows which skincare products are most preferred.

Preferred product types reveal the routines consumers may prioritise, such as cleansing, moisturising, sun protection, or targeted treatments.

8. How Does Spending Change by Age Group?

This line chart shows average monthly skincare spending across different age groups.

Spending by age group helps show whether skincare purchases are stronger among younger or older consumers. This adds a trend-style comparison to the dashboard.

9. Which Brands Receive the Most Reviews?

This chart shows which beauty brands have the highest total review counts.

Review volume can indicate stronger consumer engagement, product visibility, or greater brand presence in the Malaysian beauty market.

10. Product Price Spread by Category

This box plot shows how product prices vary within each beauty product category.

Price spread shows whether categories contain mostly affordable products, premium products, or a wide range of price points.

Metadata

Domain: Malaysian skincare and beauty consumer behaviour.

Data used: Malaysian skincare survey data, beauty product data, and regional location data.

Visual idioms used: bar chart, donut chart, scatter plot, heatmap, proportional symbol map, stacked bar chart, horizontal bar chart, line chart, and box plot.

How it works: the visualisation compares skin concerns, product categories, shopping platforms, prices, ratings, and regional spending patterns using coordinated colour scales, interactive tooltips, smooth navigation buttons, and a clickable legend in the scatter plot.

Conclusion

Overall, the dashboard shows that skincare behaviour in Malaysia is shaped by personal skin concerns, shopping convenience, product type, and regional spending differences. Consistent colour scales help users compare concerns and product categories across multiple charts.
