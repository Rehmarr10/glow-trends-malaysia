Beauty • Skincare • Malaysia

Glow Trends Malaysia

A data story on how Malaysian consumers discover, evaluate, and purchase skincare products across concerns, platforms, categories, prices, and regions.

By combining survey responses with beauty product data, this dashboard reveals what drives skincare choices in Malaysia and where the strongest consumer patterns appear.

1,200+ Survey responses analysed
8 Malaysian regions compared
10 Visualisations used
RM 223 Average skincare spending

1. Which Skin Concerns Are Most Common?

This bubble chart highlights the skincare concerns most frequently mentioned by Malaysian consumers. Larger circles represent concerns reported more often, helping identify the needs that most strongly shape skincare purchasing behaviour.

Insight: Acne, dryness, sensitivity and pigmentation appear as important skincare concerns. This suggests demand for products linked to oil control, hydration, brightening and skin-barrier repair.

2. Where Do Consumers Buy Skincare?

This donut chart compares the share of skincare purchases across major shopping channels. It shows whether consumers rely more on online platforms or pharmacy retailers when buying beauty products.

Insight: Purchase channels reveal how convenience, trust and platform access influence where Malaysian consumers choose to buy skincare.

3. Which Product Categories Receive the Best Ratings?

This lollipop chart ranks beauty product categories by average customer rating. It supports quick comparison of satisfaction levels across product types.

Insight: Rating differences help show which categories are perceived as more effective or satisfying by consumers.

4. How Do Skin Concerns Differ by Region?

This heatmap compares skincare concerns across Malaysian regions. Colour intensity shows where each concern appears more frequently, allowing regional patterns to be compared within one visual.

Insight: Regional differences suggest that skincare needs are not uniform across Malaysia. Climate, lifestyle and access to products may influence the concerns consumers report.

5. Regional Skincare Spending Map

This coded choropleth-style geographic map compares average skincare spending across selected Malaysian regions. Colour intensity represents spending levels, making regional differences easier to identify.

Insight: Sabah and Sarawak show stronger average spending, suggesting that skincare demand may vary by region rather than being evenly distributed across Malaysia.

6. How Do Product Categories Differ Across Platforms?

This stacked bar chart compares product category distributions across major shopping platforms. It highlights how different retailers may support different beauty shopping needs.

Insight: Platform differences help show whether consumers use certain channels for everyday skincare products, targeted treatments or wider product variety.

7. Which Product Types Are Preferred?

This horizontal bar chart ranks skincare product types by consumer preference. It shows which routines or product categories receive the most attention.

Insight: Preferred product types reveal the routines consumers may prioritise, such as cleansing, moisturising, sun protection or targeted treatments.

8. How Does Spending Change by Age Group?

This line chart compares average monthly skincare spending across age groups. It adds a trend-style comparison between younger and older consumers.

Insight: Spending by age group helps show whether skincare purchases are stronger among younger consumers, older consumers or remain relatively stable.

9. Which Brands Receive the Most Reviews?

This ranking chart compares beauty brands by total review count. Review volume can indicate brand visibility, consumer engagement and market presence.

Insight: Brands with higher review counts may have stronger consumer awareness, wider availability or more active online product discussion.

10. Product Price Spread by Category

This box plot shows how product prices vary within each beauty product category. It helps compare affordability, premium positioning and price range differences.

Insight: Price spread shows whether a category contains mostly affordable products, premium products or a wide range of price points.

Metadata

Domain:
Malaysian skincare and beauty consumer behaviour.

Who:
Malaysian skincare consumers, beauty retailers, marketers and product developers.

What:
Skin concerns, purchase channels, categories, prices, ratings, reviews and regional spending.

Why:
To understand what influences skincare purchases and where stronger market demand appears.

How:
Data is transformed using bubble charts, donut charts, lollipop charts, heatmaps, geographic maps, stacked bars, line charts and box plots.

Interaction:
Hover tooltips allow users to inspect values without overcrowding the page.
